250 words isn't a whole lot when you think about it, so I started thinking of a way to get around it. And a way to present my application as not just another 250 words.

And I came up with this. I used HTML / CSS, Javascript and jQuery, three coding languages that I have taught myself. To uncover the additional information, simply click the blue text. And to close the red text, click on it.



From Medium's ability to allow users to comment on individual paragraphs, Tablet's dynamic reading progress bar, and the BBC's own elegant scrolling articles, digital news outlets are constantly evolving. It's imperative that our words not only speak well, but look the part too.

This concept of additonal information has also been used in video games have used for decades. It allows the most devoted players to delve into the extended lore, whilst letting the more linear players to simply bypass it all.

something that I felt myself when I spent a week at the Daily Telegraph Culture Desk,
